# DNA model

Every creature contains a DNA profile. DNA is represented as a group of numerical traits. These traits are not meant to be a biological genome. They are a compact behavioral parameter set for an artificial life simulation.

Speed controls how fast a creature can move.

Vision controls the distance within which a creature can react to food, predators or prey.

Aggression is more important for predators and influences hunting pressure.

Sociability is more important for prey and influences local grouping behavior.

Fertility controls the probability of reproduction when energy and cooldown requirements are satisfied.

Metabolism controls how quickly energy is consumed.

Nocturnality controls whether a creature performs better during the night or during the day.

Longevity controls the approximate maximum age of a creature.

Hue controls the creature color, making inherited variation visible on screen.

When a creature reproduces, its child receives a mutated copy of the parent DNA. Mutation slightly changes some traits. Over time, this lets the population drift. If a trait helps survival and reproduction under the current environment, that trait can become more common.
