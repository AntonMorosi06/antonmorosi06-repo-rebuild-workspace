# Artificial life model

The simulation represents a simplified ecosystem.

Agents move in a two-dimensional world. Food appears in the world. Agents consume food to gain energy. Movement, metabolism, perception and body size consume energy.

Agents have genomes. A genome defines speed, perception, metabolism, size, fertility and mutation intensity.

If an agent has enough energy and survives long enough, it may reproduce. The child receives a mutated copy of the parent genome.

Agents die when they run out of energy or become too old.

This creates a basic artificial-life loop: survival, reproduction, inheritance, mutation and selection pressure.
